### ðŸ”¬ Key Concepts

- **Simple Linear Regression**  
  The goal is to model the relationship between the independent variable $X$ (cargo volume) and the dependent variable $Y$ (revenue):

  $$
  Y = \beta_0 + \beta_1 X + \varepsilon
  $$

  Where:
    - $Y$: Dependent variable (revenue)
    - $X$: Independent variable (cargo volume)
    - $\beta_0$: Intercept
    - $\beta_1$: Slope
    - $\varepsilon$: Random error term

- **Pearson Correlation Coefficient ($r$)**  
  Measures the linear relationship between $X$ and $Y$:

  $$
  r = \frac{S_{xy}}{\sqrt{S_{xx} \cdot S_{yy}}}
  $$

    - $r \in [-1, 1]$
    - A value near 1 or -1 indicates a strong correlation.

- **Sum of Squared Errors (SQE)**  
  Measures the discrepancy between actual and predicted values:

  $$
  SQE = \sum (y_i - \hat{y}_i)^2
  $$

- **Confidence Interval (CI)**  
  A 95% CI for prediction provides a range within which a future observation is expected to fall:

  $$
  \hat{y}_0 \pm t_{\alpha/2, n-2} \cdot \sqrt{s^2 \left(1 + \frac{1}{n} + \frac{(x_0 - \bar{x})^2}{S_{xx}}\right)}
  $$
